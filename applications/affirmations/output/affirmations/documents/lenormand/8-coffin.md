The Coffin, in the context of Lenormands, is fundamentally a symbol of closure and finality, representing the end of a chapter and the transition into a new beginning. It is a vessel that holds the past, often serving as a metaphor for a person's life story, a marriage, or a business venture. When one opens a coffin, the journey is acknowledged as complete, and the contents are no longer needed, signaling that the current phase of life is over. This metaphor suggests that the focus shifts entirely to the new beginning, acting as a powerful metaphor for letting go of old patterns, debts, or relationships to make room for something fresh and new. The act of opening the coffin is the catalyst for change, signifying that the current state is no longer relevant and the energy must be redirected toward the new phase of life. In this context, the Coffin Card serves as a reminder that the past is not to be feared but to be understood and integrated, serving as a sacred space for reflection where the old is acknowledged but the focus shifts to the future.
